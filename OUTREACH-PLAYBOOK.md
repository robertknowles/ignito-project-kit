# PropPath outreach playbook

The canonical sales motion for selling PropPath into the Australian investment-focused buyers' agent market (~500 agencies, ~1,500 individuals). This document is the source of truth for the in-CRM Playbook page — every template here is seeded into the database and editable from `/admin/crm/playbook`.

Supersedes the previous `OUTREACH-FLOW.md`.

---

## Principles

Re-read these before every batch of outreach.

- **Lower the ask, not the touches.** Push the product (free login, low friction), not your calendar.
- **DM text is hospitality, not sales.** Let the video do the work. Two sentences max.
- **Branch on signal, not on calendar.** Reply state is the only signal that drives the flow.
- **Wait longer than feels right.** Minimum 4 days between touches. In small markets, follow-up urgency reads as desperation.
- **One person per agency at a time.** Never message two people at the same firm in parallel.
- **Don't burn whales early.** Within each relevance tier, work smallest agencies first.
- **Real reasons only on follow-ups.** Never "just checking in."
- **Mark Dead and stop.** Eight unanswered touches makes you the founder no one returns calls from.
- **Warm intro > 20 cold messages.** Engineer them via Joshua, beta testers, and mutual connections.

---

## Australian greeting convention

For Australian men, **"Hey mate"** reads as personalised and locally fluent. It separates you from the dozens of mail-merge "Hi {firstName}" messages BAs see weekly.

For women, **"Hey [Name]"** is correct — first name only, casual register. Women in this industry respond at higher rates regardless, so the unlock isn't the greeting — it's just not getting it wrong.

Every template below has both variants. James picks the right one per recipient when sending.

## Numbers and pacing

Working backwards from the goal of **5 beta testers**, using your real conversion rate to date (2 betas from 35 videos = 5.7%):

| Stage | Volume target | Conversion |
|---|---|---|
| 5 beta testers | — | goal |
| Demo / trial bookings | ~9 | 60% to beta |
| Replies across cadence | ~18 | 50% to demo |
| Video DMs delivered | ~120 | 15% reply rate |
| LinkedIn invites sent | ~170 | ~70% reach video |

**Recommended pacing:**

- **Week 1-2:** 25-30 invites/week. Watch the funnel.
- **Week 3-4:** Ramp to 40-50/week if the numbers hold.
- **Week 5+:** Steady at 40-50/week until you hit 5 beta testers.
- **Estimated timeline:** 4-6 weeks of disciplined outreach.

**Do not** front-load 500 invites in 14 days. LinkedIn rate limits trigger at ~100-120 invites/week on Premium; your video production capacity caps around 50/week; and you'd burn the entire industry on a single un-iterated message version.

## The flow

Top-down with three branching points:

1. **Day 0 — Send blank LinkedIn connection request.**
2. **Day 1-10 fork:** Accepted? **Yes →** proceed to Day +1. **No →** send InMail at Day 10.
3. **Day +1 — Personalised video + DM.**
4. **Day +4 fork:** Replied? **Yes →** Conversation mode (exit pipeline). **No →** soft nudge.
5. **Day +14 fork:** Replied? **Yes →** Conversation mode. **No →** Remodel hook (Option B).
6. **Day +28 — Email.** Channel switch, different content.
7. **Day +30 — Mark Dead.** Stop.
8. **Day +120 — Optional re-engage** with a genuinely new reason.

## Templates

Each template has a Male variant ("Hey mate") and a Female variant ("Hey [Name]"). Pick per recipient.

### Step 1 — Day 0 · Blank LinkedIn request

**When to use:** First touch on a cold prospect. Sent from James's LinkedIn account.

**Body (both variants):** *(no message — blank invite)*

**Notes:** Blank invites accept at higher rates than pitched invites. Don't try to be charming in the connection note — it signals sales intent. Add the Day 0 personalisation note to the contact's CRM record (a specific thing about them you'll reference in the Day +1 video).

---

### Step 2 — Day 10 · InMail fallback

**When to use:** Connection request still unaccepted at Day 10. Send InMail with the same content as the Day +1 DM would have been.

**Male variant:**

> Hey mate — built something I think you'd find useful as a buyers agent. 90 seconds, no slides, tell me to piss off if it's not your thing.
>
> [Video link]

**Female variant:**

> Hey [Name] — built something I think you'd find useful as a buyers agent. 90 seconds, no slides, tell me to piss off if it's not your thing.
>
> [Video link]

**Notes:** Uses an InMail credit. LinkedIn Premium Business = 15 InMails/month. Don't dwell on the "we couldn't connect" framing — it reads as desperate.

---

### Step 3 — Day +1 · Video + DM

**When to use:** The day after they accept the connection. Send personalised 90-sec video + 2-sentence DM. The video pitches; the DM is hospitality.

**Male variant:**

> Hey mate — built something I think you'd find useful. 90 sec, no slides. Tell me to piss off if it's not your thing.

**Female variant:**

> Hey [Name] — built something I think you'd find useful. 90 sec, no slides. Tell me to piss off if it's not your thing.

**Notes:** Video script — first 15-20 sec is hyper-personal (their name, firm, the specific thing noted on Day 0). Remaining ~70 sec is the platform doing the thing that matches their work. CTA inside the video: pull, not push. "If you want to play with this on a real client of yours, happy to drop you a login — no calls, no pitch."

---

### Step 4 — Day +4 · Soft nudge

**When to use:** Day +4 after the video DM. Only if they haven't replied. One touch, then stop until Day +14.

**Male variant:**

> Hey mate — if you want to have a quick mess around with this on a real client, happy to drop you a login. Otherwise no harm done.

**Female variant:**

> Hey [Name] — if you want to have a quick mess around with this on a real client, happy to drop you a login. Otherwise no harm done.

**Notes:** Don't reference the previous message. Don't say "just bumping this up." Don't ask if they saw it. Just re-offer the value.

---

### Step 5 — Day +14 · Remodel hook (Option B)

**When to use:** Day +14 after the video DM. Only if they still haven't replied. References recent changes in the property landscape (rates, budget, tax) to frame PropPath as the tool for re-engaging existing clients.

**Male variant:**

> Hey mate — given the recent changes in the property landscape, our platform lets you remodel and re-engage all your clients' scenarios in under a minute. Worth a look if you're fielding "what does this mean for me" questions from clients right now.

**Female variant:**

> Hey [Name] — given the recent changes in the property landscape, our platform lets you remodel and re-engage all your clients' scenarios in under a minute. Worth a look if you're fielding "what does this mean for me" questions from clients right now.

**Notes:** Update "recent changes in the property landscape" to reference the most recent specific event (rate move, budget announcement, tax change) — keep it current to the week you're sending.

---

### Step 6 — Day +28 · Email

**When to use:** Day +28 after the video DM. Channel switch from LinkedIn to email. Final touch in the cadence. Different content — a case study (once available) or a budget/rate bridge version.

**Subject line options:**

- "Modelling [recent change] for investment clients in 30 seconds"
- "How [Beta tester firm] uses PropPath for [strategy]" *(once case study is ready)*

**Male variant (bridge version, no case study yet):**

> Hey mate — last note from me. Most BAs we talk to are getting client questions about how the [recent rate / budget shift] plays into their existing portfolios. We built a quick way to remodel any client's scenario in under a minute and show them the new picture.
>
> 60-sec walkthrough: [link]
>
> If you want the platform login I mentioned on LinkedIn, happy to resend so it doesn't get lost. Otherwise no further follow-ups from me — appreciate your time either way.

**Female variant (bridge version, no case study yet):**

> Hey [Name] — last note from me. Most BAs we talk to are getting client questions about how the [recent rate / budget shift] plays into their existing portfolios. We built a quick way to remodel any client's scenario in under a minute and show them the new picture.
>
> 60-sec walkthrough: [link]
>
> If you want the platform login I mentioned on LinkedIn, happy to resend so it doesn't get lost. Otherwise no further follow-ups from me — appreciate your time either way.

**Notes:** Once you have a beta tester quote, swap the middle paragraph for case-study framing: "[Beta tester name] at [agency] just used this to remodel their book — said it took ~5 minutes for 12 clients." The "no further follow-ups" line is critical — it signals the campaign is genuinely ending, which respects them and counterintuitively *increases* response rates.

---

### Step 7 — Day +120 · Optional re-engage

**When to use:** Only if you have a genuinely new reason. Otherwise, leave them alone permanently.

**Male variant:**

> Hey mate — appreciate it's been a while. [Specific new thing — e.g. "Ben at Compound just told me his clients are leaning into [X] strategy and we shipped a feature for that this week"]. Thought you might want a fresh look. No follow-ups if not — just felt it'd be wrong not to mention.

**Female variant:**

> Hey [Name] — appreciate it's been a while. [Specific new thing]. Thought you might want a fresh look. No follow-ups if not — just felt it'd be wrong not to mention.

**Notes:** If you can't fill in [Specific new thing] with a real, current, prospect-relevant fact, skip this step entirely. "Just thinking of you" sends don't qualify.

## Weekly rhythm

- **Monday morning (Rob, 60 min):** Source 30 prospects into CRM with Day 0 notes.
- **Tuesday afternoon (James, 90 min):** Batch-film personalised videos for last week's accepters.
- **Daily M-F (James, 15-20 min):** Open "what's due today" view in CRM, send the day's connection requests, DMs, and follow-ups.
- **Friday afternoon (Rob, 30 min):** Send Day +28 emails for the week's cohort. Review funnel metrics.
- **Friday evening (both, 20 min):** Quick CRM review. One adjustment for next week.

Total: ~4 hours/week distributed across both founders.

## What we are explicitly NOT doing

- Automated LinkedIn sequences (Dripify, Lemlist, MeetAlfred, Apollo cadences). LinkedIn ToS risk + brand risk + market-recognises-it risk.
- Mail merge with first-name tokens beyond the gender-appropriate greeting.
- Multi-person prospecting within the same agency in parallel.
- Asking for meetings as the primary CTA on first touch.
- Sending pitch decks unsolicited.
- "Just checking in" messages.
- More than 4 touches across 30 days per contact.
- Re-engaging Dead prospects without a real new reason.

## Iteration

The whole point of putting this in the CRM is so the templates evolve as we learn what works. After each batch of 30 outreaches, look at the funnel numbers and adjust ONE variable for the next batch (template wording, follow-up timing, personalisation depth, or hook angle). Six iterations in, you'll have a flow tuned to *your* market specifically — not the generic version.

Update the templates in the CRM directly. The version in this markdown file should be re-synced periodically as the canonical reference, but the CRM is the operational source of truth.
